1- What is the choke Adapter?

    Choke Adapter definition
  1. A choke Adapter is a component in a chokemanifold system that connects choke valve to the flow lines or other equipment .

    it ensure a secure , leak-free connection and allows the fixed choke valve to control wellbore pressure safely


    What is its Function?
  1. provides a flanged or threaded connection between the choke and flowline
  2. Allows Installation of different types or sizes of chokes without changing the rest of the manifold
  3. Supports high-pressure operations during well testing or well control
  4. Ensure smooth flow path and minimizes erosion inside the choke system

    Why it is Important?
  1. choke manifoldis critical for well testing and blowout control
  2. a properly installed choke Adapter prevents leaks and maintain pressure integrity.
  3. Adapters allow quick replacement of chokes if needed saving time during operations.

    choke Adapter use/position
  1. used in positive choke side

    explaination

    • the choke adapter is installed on the positive choke side of the choke manifold
    • the "positive choke side " refer to the side where the wellbore pressure is controlled precisely or accurately and carefully by the choke valve
    • Essentialy, choke adapter is part of the pressure control system and ensure safe and accurate flow management
  2. it is used when we have choke small bean as we install the bean inside the Adapter

    explaination

    • the choke bean is the internal part of a choke that determine flow rate of fluid or gas through the valve
    • when the choke beans are small, they cannot be installed directly in the main fixed / positive choke valve
    • chke adapter houses the small choke bean, allowing it to fit properly and function efficiently
  3. used as a trim option

    explaination

    • "Trim" refers to the ability to modify the internal flow path of the choke by changing the internal parts; such as the choke beans or sleeves
    • the choke adapter allows the choke valve to have the trim option, meaning you can change the choke beans without replacing the whole valve
    • this is usefull for fine-tuning flow rates during well testing or production without major equipment changes

    4. A choke adapter is installed on the positive choke side in the manifold, where precise pressure control is required, it is used when we have small choke beans, as the adapter houses the bean inside the choke valve. it also serves as a trim option, allowing us to adjusting internal flow path easily without replacing the entire choke

    choke Adapter Material
  • usualy high-strength steel withstands high pressures and erosive fluids

What is Choke Seat

Choke Seat desfinition
  • A choke seat is the internal part of choke valve that forms the surface against which the choke bean or plug seals. It is basically the "receiving" or "contact" surface that determines the flow restriction and allows precise control of the wellbore pressure.
  • choke seat is stationary, while choke bean or plug moves to open and close the flow
  • made of erosion-resistant steel or alloys, because high-pressure fluid can damage it
  • plays a crucial role in a well control, testing, and production operations
Choke Seat Function
  • works with choke bean or plug to control fluid gas flow
  • Ensures tight sealing to prevent leaks
  • Guides the flow through the restricted opening to maintain safe pressure downstream
  • Supports trim options in some choke systems for adjustable flow rates.
Simple Analogy
  • think of it like a faucet seal in a household tap: the valve (choke bean) presses against the seat to stop or control the flow. if the seat is damage, it leaks or cannot control the flow properly.

A choke seat is the fixed surface inside the choke valve where the choke bean or plug seals to control flow and pressure. it is critical for safe operation and allows precise flow adjustment

Choke Seat explaination
  1. Exist in the adjustable side of the choke
  2. it is used as a bean which has a tapered inside lip allowing the adjustable cone shaped end sliding in and out of it to allow flow control.

What is the Sleeve

choke sleeve
  • it is attached to the stem of the adjustable Choke and as the stem moves in and out, the sleeve show the scale of size.
  • in an adjustable choke, there is a stem (a long rod or shaft) that moves in and out to control the size of the flow opening.
  • Attached to this stem is a sleeve - a visible part that moves along with the stem. As the stem moves:
    1. Sleeve slides over a graduated scale (like a ruler) .
    2. This movement shows the operator the current size of the choke opening
    3. that helps estimate or control the flow rate of fluids passing through
  • purpose: it is like a visual indicator - instead of guessing how much the choke size is open, the sleeve tells you by showing a number or position on the scale.

Why do we install the adjustable choke on the right side?

Because the stream is flowing in a clockwise direction that may cause a sudden movement of the adjustable choke in the closing direction (Fail-Safe mode). But if we installed it on the left side it may cause the choke to move in the opening direction (Not Safety).
