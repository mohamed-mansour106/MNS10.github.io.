How does temperature and gas SG affect Gas Rate?

How does temperature and gas SG affect Gas Rate?

The Gas rate increases with increasing gas temperature, due to expansion. Because of the same gas mass with same molecules will expand to occupy a larger volume resulting to decreasing in Gas SG .

The Gas rate increases with decreasing Gas SG as light gas has high velocity.

The gas SG (density) decreases with increasing temperature (SG ~ 1/T) due to gas expansion. The gas SG (density) increases with increasing pressure (SG ~ P) due to gas high compressibility as the gas molecules are packed together (gas will occupy a smaller volume) and may approach the behavior of liquid, and the velocity of the compressed gas will decrease, so the flowmeter measurement will be low.


What is the relation between H2S amount in gas and Gas SG?

The high H2S amount in the gas, the High gas SG is This is because H2S is a heavy gas 1.189 (heavier than the air)

there is a direct relation between the H2S content in gas and Gas Specific Gravity
Explaination:
  • H2S is a heavier gas comparing to the main component of th natural gas, which is usually Methane (CH4)
    • Molecular weight of H2S = 34 g/mol
    • Molecular weight of CH4 = 12 g/mol
Relation:
  • As the H2S content increases, the average Molecular weight of the gas increases.
  • This causes the Gas SG to increases.

How to measure Gas SG?

    Gas Specific Gravity is measured by Comparing the density of gas to density og air, here is how it is commonly done
  1. using a gas chromatograph (GC)
    • Step1:Collect a gas Sample
    • Step2:Run it through a gas chromatograph
    • Step3:GC gives the composition of the gas percentage of methane, H2S, ethane, Co2
    • Step4:use the composition to calculate gas SG. based on molecular weights.
  2. Using a Gas Gravimeter (Lab Device)
    • measures the density of gas directly
    • then: Gas SG= Gas density / Air density at the same condition (Air density at standard condition = 1.225 kg/m3 )
    • Approximate Field Estimation (less Accurate)
      • if only Co2 or H2S is added to sweet gas, you can estimate SG using:
        SG = ‚àë (fraction of component X molecular weight of component) / 28.96

      • you can estimate gas SG based on gas composition, particulrly H2S and Co2 content, as they increase SG. you can use this Approximate formula if you have mole % (or volume %) of components like CH4, CO2, H2S

        Gas SG = ‚àë (Yi X Mi) / 28.96
          where
        • Yi= mole fraction of component i
        • Mi= molecular weight of component i
        • 28.96= molecular weight of air

          Example: Assume dry gas with:

        • CH4: 90%, CO2:6%, H2S:4%.
        • SG = [(0.90x16) + (0.06x44) + (0.04x34)] / 28.96
        • SG = (14.4 + 2.64 + 1.36) / 28.96
          • Methane (CH4) = 16.04 g/mol
          • Carbon Dioxide (CO2) = 44.01 g/mol
          • Hydrogen Sulfide (H2S) =34.08 g/mol

What is the Procedure for Ranarex?

    Used to measure Gas Specific Gravity.
  1. Connect 1/4" SS liner tubing to top part of separator gas line.
  2. Flush SS liner with separator gas, to ensure no air contamination
  3. Connect the stainless-steel sample Cylinder (with two isolation valve) to the SS liner.
  4. Crack opens the isolating valve on the sample liner and fill the Cylinder.
    5. Noted: Do not over-pressurize the Cylinder (0-15)psi as it is not a pressurized device and will rupture if excess pressure is applied.
  5. Vent the gas in the Cylinder in order to flush the container and repeat this 2 times
  6. Fill the Cylinder with gas and measure the specific Gravity using the RANAREX Gravitometer

    Answer: ‚úÖ A2: We need to keep monitoring annular pressure to.
