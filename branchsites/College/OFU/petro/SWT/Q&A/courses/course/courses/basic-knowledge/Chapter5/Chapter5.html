  1. Q1: what is Annulus ?

    Answer: ‚úÖ A1: Annulus is the void between any piping, tubing or casing strings .

    In completed well, we have many annuli:-

    • Annulus (A): is the void between the tubing and the smallest casing string 95/8". It can serve in some tasks like gas lifting and well killing.
    • Annulus (B): is the void between the production casing string and the intermediate casing string 133/8"
    • Annulus (C):

    Annulus is usualy filled with brine

  2. Q2: why we need to keep monitoring annular pressure?

    Answer: ‚úÖ A2: We need to keep monitoring annular pressure to.

    • prevent excessive Annular pressure build-up (APD) due to brine expansion because of temperature increase while flowing the well. which lead to casing failure, collapse or formation damage
    • maintain pressure in Annulus to keep well integrity of the casing string to avoid Ballon-Effect.
  3. Q3: how can we keep annular pressure balance ?

    Answer: ‚úÖ A3: we have to cases :

    • if the annulus pressure is decreasing, we have to pump more brine in the annulas to maintain pressure
    • if the annulus pressure is increasing, at surface pressure can be relieved by opening casing valve and venting pressure to a tank.
  4. Q4: what is the reason for the disbalance of the annulus pressure?

    Answer: ‚úÖ A4: the extra reasons for APD Annulus Pressure Build-up are:

    • packer seal leak
    • poor cementing job
  5. Q5: what is DHSV?

    Answer: ‚úÖ A5: DHSV (Down Hole Safety Valve ) is a safety device installed in the upper part of the wellbore to provide emmergency closure to the well in the event of the worst-case-scenario surface disaster.
