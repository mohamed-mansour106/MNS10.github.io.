Introduction to Well Stimulation, Dr. Ahmed Algarhy

lecture 17

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Introduction and Background (00:02 - 01:50): Dr. Ahmed Algarhy is introduced, highlighting his expertise and research in well stimulation and hydrocarbon recovery. The session marks the beginning of a course on well stimulation, with quizzes after each lecture.
  2. Formation Damage (03:56 - 15:59): Dr. Algarhy explains the importance of porosity and permeability in reservoirs, detailing how damage during drilling, cementing, and other operations can reduce permeability and affect production. He introduces the concept of "skin" and how formation damage impacts oil and gas recovery.
  3. Formation Damage and Chemical Effects (11:48 - 18:10): The lecture covers how chemical reactions, like changes in wettability, can negatively affect oil and gas production, leading to water production instead of hydrocarbons.
  4. Hydraulic Fracturing (36:39 - 42:25): Dr. Algarhy discusses hydraulic fracturing as a method to enhance production by creating fractures in the formation, with stages involved in this process. The lecture clarifies the role of proppants and fracture fluids in keeping fractures open.
  5. Well Stimulation Techniques and Challenges (49:44 - 1:20:11): The final part focuses on the use of hydraulic fracturing for sand control, the importance of constant injection rates during stimulation, and the difference between fixing formation damage and bypassing it. The lecture concludes with the need to properly diagnose issues before applying well stimulation techniques.
