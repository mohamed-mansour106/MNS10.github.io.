Keys to success in the oil and gas industry, advice to students from Dr. Ahmed Algarhy

lecture 20

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Career Path and Resilience: Dr. Ahmed Algarhy shares his unconventional journey from a desire to study IT to becoming a prominent figure in the oil and gas industry. His father’s influence led him to engineering, and his journey involved overcoming setbacks, including an economic downturn, job challenges, and visa obstacles, which taught him resilience and adaptability.
  2. Educational Advice and Skill Development: He emphasizes the importance of continually building qualifications, suggesting students pursue a master’s or Ph.D. if job opportunities are scarce. He highlights the value of technical knowledge and hands-on experience, stressing that a solid work ethic, adaptability, and a proactive approach to learning are essential.
  3. Networking and Work Experience: Dr. Algarhy credits much of his success to networking and work experience, which helped compensate for lower academic performance. He advises students to cultivate strong relationships, pursue internships, and volunteer with industry associations like SPE or SEG to expand their professional networks.
  4. Professional Conduct and Job Stability: Dr. Algarhy stresses reliability, punctuality, and teamwork, advising against job-hopping without a backup plan. He notes that employers value consistent performance and positive attitudes, especially in challenging market conditions, as these traits make employees indispensable.
  5. Adapting to Industry Cycles and Economic Realities: He advises students to prepare for the cyclical nature of the oil and gas industry, emphasizing the importance of saving money and acquiring skills that remain in demand regardless of oil prices. He expresses optimism about post-pandemic opportunities, advising students to stay ready for unexpected openings.
