The Skin Effect for Petroleum Reservoirs

lecture 23

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Introduction to Skin Effect (00:00-00:37): The skin effect is a dimensionless factor used to explain the difference between ideal and actual well performance, influenced by the resistance around the wellbore due to drilling and completion methods.
  2. Positive vs. Negative Skin (00:37-02:08): Positive skin indicates damaged formations with reduced permeability, causing higher pressure drop and lower flow rates. Negative skin represents stimulated formations with increased permeability, leading to lower pressure drop and higher flow rates.
  3. Formation Damage and Stimulation (02:41-05:34): Formation damage is caused by drilling fluid invasion, mud cake formation, and cement invasion. Stimulation, such as acidizing or hydraulic fracturing, can reduce skin factor by increasing permeability and enhancing flow.
  4. Open Hole vs. Cased Well (06:07-06:41): Open hole wells have lower skin factors due to fewer formation invasions but may face risks of fluid entry. Cased wells have higher skin factors due to cement and completion processes.
  5. Skin Effect and Flow Rate (07:47-09:46): The skin factor directly influences flow rate—positive skin decreases production by lowering permeability, while negative skin increases flow by enhancing permeability, ultimately affecting production efficiency and costs.
