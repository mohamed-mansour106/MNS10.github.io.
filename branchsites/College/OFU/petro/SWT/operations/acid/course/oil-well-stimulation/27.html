3 Phase Test Separator

lecture 27

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Three-Phase Separator Purpose and Components: A three-phase separator is used to separate well fluids (oil, water, and gas) into their individual components through gravity, using configurations that may be either horizontal or vertical.
  2. Horizontal Separator Operation: In a horizontal separator, fluid hits an inlet diverter, causing initial gas-liquid separation. The vessel allows time for oil and emulsion to form a layer above water, with a weir and interface controller managing oil and water levels respectively. The gas exits through a mist extractor.
  3. Vertical Separator Operation: In a vertical separator, fluid enters through the side, and an inlet diverter separates the gas. Liquid flows through a downcomer, and a chimney equalizes pressure. Oil and water separate as oil rises and water flows downward in counter-current flows.
  4. Separator Type Selection: Horizontal separators handle large gas volumes and offer better phase separation but need more space and handle solids poorly. Vertical separators handle solids better, need less space, and have greater liquid surge capacity but are harder to service.
  5. Design and Operational Considerations: Separator design must account for potential issues like foaming, paraffin buildup, and sand accumulation. Proper sizing is essential, with guidelines available in the accompanying text for design theory, example exercises, and necessary formulas.
