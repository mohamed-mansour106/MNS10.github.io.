Acidizing and Formation Damage, Dr. Ahmed Gomaa

lecture 3

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Introduction to Dr. Ahmed Gomaa (00:01): Dr. Ahmed Gomaa, a fracturing fluid engineer at BP, shares his extensive experience in petroleum engineering, hydraulic fracturing, and acid stimulation. He has multiple patents, publications, and conference papers.
  2. Acidizing Fundamentals (02:11): Acidizing is used to reduce formation damage, primarily involving hydrochloric acid (HCl) and hydrofluoric acid (HF) to treat issues like scale and formation plugging. It involves complex fluid dynamics, such as spherical flow in formations.
  3. Types of Acid Treatments (07:43): Acid treatments vary by formation type—matrix acidizing for sandstone and acid fracturing for carbonates. Acidizing methods and additives are carefully selected based on formation compatibility, with common choices being HCl and organic acids.
  4. Acid Additives and Compatibility (14:59): HCl is widely used due to its reactivity with carbonate formations but requires additives to control corrosion and manage iron precipitation. HF is typically blended with HCl to prevent secondary reactions that could cause blockages.
  5. Diverting Agents and Treatment Techniques (56:51): Diverting agents are used to ensure even acid distribution in formations. Techniques like matrix acidizing and acid fracturing enhance wellbore productivity by etching and creating conductive channels in the rock formation.
