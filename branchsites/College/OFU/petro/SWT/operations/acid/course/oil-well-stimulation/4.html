Acidizing and Formation Damage

lecture 1

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Introduction to Dr. Ahmed Ghomah’s Expertise Dr. Ahmed Ghomah, a fracturing fluid engineer at BP, specializes in acid stimulation, fluid diversion, and treatment design. He has extensive experience, including work with Baker Hughes and Saudi Aramco, and holds numerous patents and publications in petroleum engineering.
  2. Acidizing Processes and Their Applications Acidizing is a process used to improve well productivity by removing formation damage near the wellbore. Acid types used include hydrochloric acid (HCl), hydrofluoric acid (HF), and organic acids, each selected based on the formation's mineral composition and specific damage issues, like scale or clay damage.
  3. Types of Acid Treatments There are various acidizing treatments: matrix acidizing (low-pressure injection to dissolve minerals near the wellbore), acid fracturing (high-pressure injection to create fractures), and pickling (scale removal). Matrix acidizing is suitable for carbonate formations, while HF-based treatments address sandstone damage.
  4. Challenges and Additives in Acidizing Key challenges include managing corrosion (especially with HCl), preventing precipitation, and controlling acid reaction rates. Additives like corrosion inhibitors, iron control agents, and diverters are crucial for effective acid treatment, enhancing acid placement and minimizing potential formation damage.
  5. Acidizing Tools and Techniques Acid placement methods, such as coiled tubing and packers, help direct acid precisely within the formation. Mechanical methods are used to control acid flow, ensuring effective treatment while minimizing costs.
