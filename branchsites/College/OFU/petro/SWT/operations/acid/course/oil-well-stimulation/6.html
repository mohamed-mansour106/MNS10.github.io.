lecture 6

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Introduction to Acidizing and Completions (00:05–00:48): Kyle Travis, a petroleum engineer, introduces the concept of completions and workovers, focusing on acidizing and stimulation in wells, particularly matrix acidizing, acid fracking, and acid washes as part of completions.
  2. Matrix Acidizing and Considerations (00:48–02:08): Matrix acidizing is effective for carbonate and sandstone formations. Key considerations include maintaining pressure below frack parting pressure and avoiding excess acid to prevent damage such as casing collapse or sand production.
  3. Types of Acidizing Jobs (02:08–02:52): Different acid jobs include matrix acid jobs, acid washes to remove scale deposits, and acid soaks to clean perforations. Acid fracking is limited to carbonate formations, and various acids like hydrochloric, hydrofluoric, and organic acids are used.
  4. Properties of Acids Used in Stimulation (02:52–05:36): Mineral acids like hydrochloric and hydrofluoric acid are commonly used, with concentrations ranging from 1–28%. Organic acids are slower-reacting and are used in high-temperature wells. Hybrid acids combine both types for optimal performance.
  5. Acid Reactions and Challenges (05:36–09:37): The reaction of acids with rock matrices varies, with certain combinations potentially damaging formations. Acid treatments, particularly for sandstone, may involve mud acids to dissolve clay materials, but care must be taken to avoid damaging reactions, such as the one between hydrofluoric acid and calcium carbonate.
