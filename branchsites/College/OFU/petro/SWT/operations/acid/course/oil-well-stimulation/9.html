Hydraulic Fracturing Stimulation

lecture 9

    Here’s a summary of acid stimulation in oil wells based on various sources:
  1. Initial Drilling and Casing (00:04–00:47): The well is drilled to a designated depth, ensuring protection for freshwater sources. Surface casing is inserted and cemented to prevent contamination of aquifers.
  2. Drilling and Mud Logging (01:31–02:09): Drilling continues through the intermediate section, with mud pumped to keep the drill cool, carry cuttings, and provide pressure. A mud logger analyzes cuttings for hydrocarbon presence.
  3. Logging and Casing Installation (02:49–03:27): After drilling to target depth, a logging tool gathers data, creating an electric log. Production casing is installed and cemented, and the wellhead is prepared for production.
  4. Perforation and Hydraulic Fracturing (03:27–04:03): The casing is perforated with a gun to create holes for hydraulic fracturing. High-pressure sand and fluid are pumped into the formation to fracture the target zones and allow oil to flow.
  5. Final Equipment Installation and Production (04:44–end): Bridge plugs isolate fractured zones, and production tubing is installed. The wellhead, surface equipment, and pipeline are set up for oil transport, optimizing productivity from multiple zones.
