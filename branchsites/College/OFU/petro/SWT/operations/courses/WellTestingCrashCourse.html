Well Testing Introduction

In this lecture, you will get a brief background history of Well Testing, learn the definition and the purpose of Well Testing, understand the types and the sequences of Well Testing, and finally, know what are the required measurements from Well Testing.

brief of the Courses:

client's questions
  1. what is the size of the reservoir?
  2. how much will it produce?
  3. what fluids are going to produce?
what is the well testing?
  • well testing is the omly technique that examines a significant portion of the reservoir under dynamic condition to determine production capability and reservoir properties.
Data acquired when the reservoir is tested:-
  1. flow rate
  2. pressure and temprature
  3. PVT samples of representative reservoir fluids
Sequences of well testing:-
  • in oreder to produce oil at surface, TCV = Tubing Convey Perforation
  • DST = Drill Stem Testing , flow to surface {packer, tester valve, Tubing, SubSurface Safety Valve}
  • Surface well testing to handle flow at surface {Flowhead, Choke manifold, Burner}
  • the required measurements from well testing are:-
    1. rate of the fluid produced
    2. Bottom hole conditions behavior
    3. PVT study of representative reservoir samplies
the purpose of DST
  1. Determine the name of the fluid produced
    • PVT analysis performed at bottom hole condition or surface recombined samples.
  2. Define the well productivity
    • productivity index and IPR plot for oil wells
    • Deliverbility curve and Absolute Open Flow {AOF} for gas wells.
  3. Evaluate the characterestics of the producing formation.
    • static pressure (PI)
    • Formation Flow Capacity (Kh) heterogeneity, limits.
  4. Evaluate any formation damage (Skin factor, S)
    • Determine if acidizing or other treatment is required.
    • control the result of the simulation or treatment.
