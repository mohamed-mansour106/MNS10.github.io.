Chapter 4 SWT Overview

 

Creation of a pressure distribance depends on whether the reservoir is producing or shut-in

  • If the well has been flowing gor a long time, is to flow the reservoir, this is called drawdown.
  • If the well has been flowing for a long time, is to shut in the well, this is called buildup .

 

The flowhead

The flowhead controls the well pressure.

 

The ChokeManifold

The chokemanifold controls the flow and the pressure.

 

The heater or steam exchanger

the heater or steam exchanger is used to raise the effluent temperature to fight hydrates (gas well) and to break emulsion or reduce foam and viscosity (oil well) and improve burning.

 

The separator

the separator is used to separate meter and sample the three phases of the effluent.

 

The guage or surge tank

Thguage or surge tank is used to store oil to calibrate the liquid meters, to measure shrinkage and low liquid flow rate.

 

The Transfer pump

The transfer pump is used to empty the tank and to ensure promper atomization pressure (clean burner) at the burner head .

 

The oil burner

The oil is disposed of through the burner located at the extremity of the booms to reduce heat radiations towards the rig .

 

The gas flare

The gas is burner separately through a gas flare located on the burner booms .

 

The various of the SWT chain are interconnected with piping; Flexible Coflexip between flowhead and chokemanifold, and rigid piping elswhere .

 

The Coflexip

  • high pressure flexible hose, Coflexip, is used between the flowhead and chokemanifold, to allow up and down movement of the DST string .
  • long length available (40ft, 60ft) less number of seals and risks of rupture or leak.
  • Coflexip hoses are heavy, and te use of a crane is mandatory to rig up safely.
  • to respect the MBR (Minimum Bending Radius) a couple of elbows, maybe used in ends of both sides
.

 

The Rigid piping

Rigid piping with weco-union connections or graylock for high pressure lines.

straight piping, often 1m, or 5m, or 3m long.

elbows, often 90 or 45 degree
