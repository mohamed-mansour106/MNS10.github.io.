What is DST?

(openhole, Barefoot, Casedhole, straddle test)

  • Drill Stem Testing is a temporary completions with different combinations of downhole tools run on drill pipe or tubing.
  • DST is used equally to control the well during dynamic test operations

 

Combinations of DST and functions of every equipment

  1. the String:-
    • channels the flow to surface.
  2. the Packer:- (flexpac, positreive, positest)
    • is a rubber element that isolate the formation from the annulus.
  3. Tester Valve:-
    (multi flow evaluator, pressure controlled testing PCT, Intellegent Remote System)
    • provides a method of controlling the well near of the reservoir.
    • control the formation.
    • shut the downhole to minimize wellbore storage effect.
    • isolates annular fluid cushion while RIH preventing U-tubing.
    • and provides a seal for pressure test the string.

 

Steps for ending DST:-

  1. open the reverse circulating valve
  2. close the reverse circulating valve
  3. open the tester valve
  4. pump mud into test string to kill the test interval
  5. unseat the Packer
  6. pull the string out of the hole

During the initial phase of the test of the wellbore, and later, the drilling fluid (mud)that has invaded the formation in the vicinity of the wellbore, flow to the surface. THIS IS KNOWN AS THE CLEAN-UP PERIOD

the CLEAN-UP is complete when the well effluent at surface is reservoir fluid that contains no mud particles or cutting at surface.

once cleanup complete, the main flow period can be maintained for the planned duration, during which downhole pressure measurements and surface flow rates are recorded.

at the end of the main flow period, the tester vlave is closed to buildup the formation pressure while downhole pressure measurements continues.
