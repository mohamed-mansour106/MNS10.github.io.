Chapter (1) Introduction to drilling engineer

By the end of this module, you will be able to: Describe the life-cycle of a field or reservoir and the role of the drilling engineer in each phase of production. Describe the personnel on a rig crew and personnel at the rig site. Describe the different types of drilling rigs. Understand the well and geological profiles. List the different reservoirs rock types. Understand the drilling cost, and the drilling data.

Chapter (2-1) Drilling Rig Components

Learning Objectives: By the end of this lesson, you should be able to identify and describe the five major systems of a drilling rig: the Power System, the Rotary System, the Hoisting System, the Circulating System, and the Well Control System or Blowout Prevention System.

Chapter (2-2) Drill Stem Testing Overview

By the end of this lecture, you will be able to understand the DST concept, identify the different types of DST and learn about the main basic DST equipments and the relationship between the main DST pressures.

Chapter (3) Tubing Conveyed Perforation Overview

By the end of this lecture, you will be able to understand the TCP concept, the TCP applications, the purpose of TCP and the different types of TCP guns and charges.

Chapter (4) Surface Well Testing Overview

By the end of this lecture, you will be able to understand the surface well testing concept, identify the different types of surface well testing equipments and piping.

Chapter (5) Well Testing Layout

By the end of this lecture, you will learn and identify the different types of well testing layouts for onshores and offsores rigs, and be able to understand the requirements for each type.

Chapter (7) Types of Well Tests

By the end of this lecture, you will learn and understand the main different types of well tests used in the oilfield industry.

Chapter (8) Well Testing Sequences

By the end of this lecture, you will learn and identify the different sequences of a Well Testing job.

Chapter (9) The Clean-Up Period

By the end of this lecture, you will learn and identify the different sequences of a Well Testing job.

Chapter (10) The Well Test Data

By the end of this lecture, you will learn about the list of data collected during a well testing job, identify the tools and equipment used to collect these data, and understand the results obtained from the well testing data.

Chapter (11) Productivity

By the end of this lecture, you will be able to calculate the productivity index PI, plot the inflow performance relation IPR curve, and determine the maximum flow potential or absolute open flow AOF for an oil well and a gas well.

Chapter (7) Well Testing Layout

By the end of this lecture, you will learn and identify the main safety measures to be followed in order to conduct a safe Well Testing operation for personnel and equipment.
