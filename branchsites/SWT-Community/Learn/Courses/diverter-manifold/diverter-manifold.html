Q&A Summary

    Sure! Here's a Q&A format based on the video content "SWT - Oil and Gas Manifolds":
  1. Q1: What is the purpose of oil and gas manifolds in oilfield operations?

    Answer: ✅ A: They are used to divert oil or gas from the separator to various destinations such as burners, storage tanks, or production lines without interrupting the flow..

  2. Q2: Why are two burners (port and starboard) used in offshore operations?

    Answer: ✅ A: To allow continuous testing regardless of wind direction, enabling safe disposal of oil or gas..

  3. Q3: How is oil routed through the oil manifold system?

    Answer: ✅ A: Oil from the separator is routed through the manifold to:

    • Burners for disposal
    • Storage tanks
    • Flow lines (possibly for reinjection)
  4. Q4: What is the structure of the oil manifold?

    Answer: ✅ A: It consists of five 2-inch ball valves, is skid mounted, and has a working pressure of 1440 PSI..

  5. Q5: How does the gas manifold function?

    Answer: ✅ A: It diverts gas from the separator to either the port or starboard gas flare, depending on the wind direction. It includes two 3-inch ball valves and is also skid mounted with a 1440 PSI rating..

  6. Q6: What safety practices should be followed when operating the manifolds?

    Answer: ✅ safety practices should be followed when operating the manifolds include:

    • Label all valves to indicate correct flow paths (e.g., “separator to starboard burner”).
    • Always open a new valve before closing an old one to prevent flow interruption and pressure buildup.
    • Use only the provided handles—no cheaters—to operate the valves safely.
  7. Q7: What should you do if you need to operate the valves?

    Answer: ✅ A: Use the provided handles only, and avoid using any additional tools or equipment that could damage the valves or create safety hazards..
